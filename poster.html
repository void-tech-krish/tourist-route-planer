1. Introduction

The Tourist Route Planner is an advanced hybrid AI system designed to optimize travel itineraries across India. It addresses the complex problem of travel planning by considering multiple constraints such as budget, time, and unpredictable conditions like weather and traffic.

By integrating uninformed/informed search, constraint satisfaction, adversarial reasoning, and probabilistic models, the system guarantees optimal, robust, and personalized travel decisions.

4. AI Algorithms and Their Uses

1. Uninformed & Informed Search

Algorithm: Backtracking Search

Uses: Used for core path-finding between cities (e.g., Delhi to Udaipur) while strictly adhering to user-defined constraints like a maximum travel distance or budget.

2. Constraint Satisfaction Problems (CSP)

Algorithm: Forward Checking

Uses: Enhances the search process by looking ahead to avoid dead ends (like reaching a city with no outbound routes), significantly speeding up itinerary generation.

3. Adversarial Search

Algorithms: Minimax & Alpha-Beta Pruning

Uses: Models travel planning as a game against 'nature'. It finds the safest routes by minimizing delays against worst-case scenarios (like severe bad weather blocking roads).

4. Probabilistic Models

Algorithms: Expected Utility & Bayesian Networks

Uses: Calculates the Expected Utility of scenic vs. highway routes under uncertain weather. Bayesian Networks predict the precise probability of travel delays based on traffic and weather conditions.

5. Generative AI & Natural Language

Algorithm: LLM Integration (Travelora Chatbot)

Uses: Employs Google's Gemini Pro to provide an intuitive, conversational interface, allowing users to plan trips simply by chatting in natural language.
